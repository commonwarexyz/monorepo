Reducing Block Time (and Resource Usage) with Buffered Signatures

By Patrick O'Grady
May 28, 2025

A few months ago, we launched Alto, a minimal (and wicked fast) blockchain for continuously benchmarking the Commonware Library. Today, I'm excited to share that this benchmarking drove a 20% reduction in block time (to ~200ms), a 20% reduction in finality (to ~300ms), a 65% reduction in CPU usage, and a 27% reduction in bandwidth usage.

To reason about these improvements, let's first take a step back (all the way back). Last August, the first Commonware Library primitive was released: commonware-p2p.

As a wise man once said, "If you ain't first, you're last."

Normally, need to verify each message incoming because you don't know who/where its coming from.

With authenticated peers, can buffer and block (can block with normal gossip p2p but peer can just get a new address and continue).

Wait for quorum of messages to be buffered and then verify.

If invalid, perform bisection over buffered messages until find invalid signature and block.

65% reduction in CPU (while still lowering block time).
