Minimmit: A Minimal Specification for Propose-and-Vote Finality

By Patrick O'Grady
June 20, 2025

Over the last few weeks, there has been a renewed interest in propose-and-vote consensus (under a ~20% adversary rather than the typical ~33% adversary explored in modern consensus constructions). Typically referred to as "Two-Phase" or "Two-Round" protocols (i.e. propose block and vote block), these constructions seek to "commit" (i.e. finalize) in ~100-200ms rather than the ~300-500ms achieved by most "Three-Phase" protocols (i.e. propose block, vote block, and finalize block). The cost? Less resilience to Byzantine adversaries.

Most heard of this line of research when Anza Labs released Alpenglow at Solana Accelerate a few weeks ago. However, teams at both Offchain Labs and Supra Research/Espresso Systems have also proposed constructions over the last few weeks that explore different constructions. Shared among all is a desire to surpass the simple n = 5f + 1 construction proposed in Fast Byzantine Consensus with one that tolerates some number of crashed nodes as well (with a "slow path" that can continue to make progress under certain faults).
